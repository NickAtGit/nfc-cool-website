Your iPhone is the scanner. Your Mac is the browser.

Starting today, the NFC.cool iOS and iPadOS app is compatible with Mac — install it directly from the Mac App Store, just like any other iPhone/iPad app on Apple silicon.

No separate Mac app. No subscription upgrade. Same app, one more screen.

### The sidebar — built for bigger screens

NFC.cool has always been mobile-first. On iPhone, a single column makes sense. On iPad and Mac, it wastes space.

The new **sidebar navigation** gives you quick access to all your content categories:

- **NFC tags** you've scanned
- **QR codes & Barcodes** you've captured
- **Documents** you've scanned
- **3D models & Rooms** you've scanned

Everything is one click away. Same experience on iPad — whether you're holding it or it's on your desk.

### iCloud sync — same library, every device

Every scan you've done on iPhone — NFC tags, QR codes, barcodes, documents, 3D models, room scans — syncs to your Mac automatically through **iCloud**.

Open the app on your Mac, and your entire library is already there. No export, no manual transfer. It just appears.

You can scan on the go with your iPhone, then review and organize on your Mac. Reference a tag or document while working without reaching for your phone. Same privacy approach as always: scans stay on your devices and in your iCloud. NFC.cool never sees them.

### Bonus: your Mac camera is a QR & barcode scanner

Hold a QR code up to your Mac's camera and NFC.cool reads it. Same for barcodes. Useful for quickly looking up a product barcode while you're working, or scanning a QR code from a screen without pulling out your phone.

### What doesn't work on Mac (and why)

- **NFC scanning** — Macs don't have NFC radio hardware. You need your iPhone.
- **Document scanning** — Mac cameras lack the auto-focus + edge-detection pipeline.
- **3D scanning & room scans** — these need LiDAR (iPhone Pro / iPad Pro).

Hardware constraints, not software limitations. But you probably don't need them on Mac anyway — you scan with your iPhone because it's in your hand, and you browse on Mac because it has the big screen.
