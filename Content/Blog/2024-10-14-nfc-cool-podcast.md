We recorded a short presentation of NFC.cool as a podcast - an audio walkthrough rather than a typed feature list. It covers what NFC actually is, where it sits in the daily-life tech stack, and how NFC.cool turns it into something you can hand to someone with a tap.

If you've ever wondered what the difference is between Apple Pay-style NFC and the random NFC sticker on a museum exhibit, that's the kind of thing the episode unpacks - with concrete examples, not whitepaper jargon.

**Listen on [SoundCloud](https://soundcloud.com/nfc-cool)**.

Topics on the episode include:

- The two-mode NFC protocol: who's powered, who's responding, why it matters
- Where NFC.cool Tools sits between the OS and the hardware
- The cross-platform plumbing behind the NFC.cool Business Card (App Clip on iOS, web on Android)
- The roadmap: what's next on iPhone, on Android, on Mac
- Practical NFC use cases we keep seeing in the wild

It's a good listen if you prefer audio to documentation, or if you're commuting and want a 20-minute primer on what we're building.
